01
Who we are

ELS CAFÈS DEL MANEL
A way of being

Els Cafès del Manel is a specialty coffee brand rooted in Catalonia that understands coffee as a cultural, shared experience. It is not just a drink, it is a way of being and connecting.

It was born at a time when coffee has become a trend and often an aesthetic. We offer an alternative with criteria and its own identity, focused on quality and a narrative coherent with the territory.

Without unnecessary pretence or elitism.

The project is built around three pillars that give each other meaning:
Coffee, with a selection of origins made with rigour.
Culture, as a natural space for gathering and creation.
Community, because we believe in conversation, closeness, and the bonds that form around a table.

"Coffee is not for the photo.
It's for the conversation that follows."
Els Cafès del Manel

Vision & criteria

We select coffees with rigour and coherence, seeking soul and purpose. We are not driven by volume, but by identity.

Every coffee that reaches us has a story behind it, and you can taste it in the cup.

Community before consumption

Coffee is, above all, a meeting point. The conversation matters as much as what you drink.

We care about creating spaces where connection is real and where shared time carries real weight.

Honesty & transparency

We steer clear of pretence and artifice that complicate the simple. Good coffee shouldn't create distance; it should bring people closer.

It should be knowledge, tradition, and shared pleasure, without barriers.

Catalan identity, open vision

We are born in Catalan and think from the territory, with the will to engage with the world.

We do not want to seem from everywhere; we are from here, and that is part of our positioning.

Living culture

Coffee connects with art, music, and contemporary creation. It's not decoration, but context.

A cultural experience that connects people and ideas.

Quality with a story

Every product reflects a story and a way of understanding coffee that goes beyond taste.

If we do it, we do it right, with purpose and respect for what's behind it.

02
Our philosophy

COFFEE, CULTURE
& Community

We understand coffee as culture, and culture as a space that is lived and shared. Els Cafès del Manel was not born just to sell coffee, but to grow something around it.

A project where identity, quality and community walk together, without separating.

We would love it if, when someone thinks of coffee with Catalan identity or of gatherings that connect coffee and creation, they naturally think of Els Cafès del Manel. That the name comes up on its own, without being forced.

Mission

To grow Els Cafès del Manel as a specialty coffee brand with a clear identity rooted in Catalan culture.

We want to bring specialty coffee closer to more people and remove the label of exclusivity.

We promote gatherings where coffee blends with art and music, and we work to create a community that lives it as a shared experience, not as a trend.

Vision

To become a reference for specialty coffee in Barcelona and Catalonia, not only for what we serve but for the way we do it.

We aspire that when someone thinks of coffee with Catalan identity and of projects that connect coffee and culture, they think of Els Cafès del Manel naturally.

We do not want to be just a coffee brand. We want to be a project with roots and a journey.

Values

Catalan identity: We are from here, in language and in the way we work, and from here we open up to the world.

Coffee is for everyone: Good coffee should not create distance. It should be accessible and easy to understand.

Community: Coffee is a meeting point and conversation is part of the experience.

Criteria: We choose our coffees with rigour and coherence. Every decision has a reason.

Culture: Coffee is part of a cultural context that includes art, music and thought.

Three origins, three streets

A tribute to Gràcia

Gràcia is not just a neighbourhood, it is a way of living and understanding the rhythm of the day. Our coffees are not just origins either; they have their own character and personality.

Each coffee in the collection bears the name of a street in Gràcia that reflects its character.
Three origins and three ways of expressing themselves in the cup, united by the same rigour and a clear way of understanding coffee.

VERDI

Brazil · Fazenda São Pedro
The balance of everyday - "Like Verdi street, it's a coffee that makes you feel at home"

Verdi street is a place where life flows without rush. Full terraces, the old cinema, people who greet each other by name. It is welcoming, warm and constant.

Brazil is exactly that in the cup.

A sweet, round and gentle coffee. Natural, with a medium body and notes of chocolate and almond. It does not seek stridency or extreme complexity. It seeks to do things well. Every day.

It comes from Fazenda São Pedro, in the Alta Mogiana region, Santo Antonio do Jardim, Brazil. The red-clay soil and the altitude of 980 metres give the bean its characteristic sweetness and balanced body.

The Bourbon Amarelo variety is one of the most prized in Brazil for its sweet and complex profile. Processed using the natural method, the cherry dries with the mucilage fully intact, allowing sugars to concentrate during aerobic fermentation, lending the honey-like texture and red fruit notes that define this coffee.

Gran de Gràcia is the main artery of the neighbourhood. It connects, organises and gives structure. It has movement, but also balance. It is classic, yet full of personality.

The Golden Huila from Colombia has that same presence.

A balanced, structured and refined coffee. Pronounced sweetness, clean acidity and a creamy body. Notes of milk chocolate and brown sugar with a citric touch that brings light. It is not an extreme coffee. It is a solid coffee.

Golden Huila is produced by a cooperative in the Huila coffee region, a project of more than 3,000 coffee-growing families from central Huila. The beans are carefully selected by young people, children of coffee growers who have trained to complement the knowledge inherited through years of family experience in the world of coffee farming.

Torrent de l'Olla has a different energy. It does not call for attention. It opens slowly, in layers.

Papua New Guinea is exactly that in the cup.

Sweet and floral, clean and deep. Notes of vanilla, caramel and jasmine with dark chocolate in the background. A surprising balance between perfect sweetness and a vibrant acidity that brings life. Not what you expect. What you discover.

The Sigri Coffee Estate plantation is located in the Wahgi Valley, in the heart of the Central Highlands of New Guinea. Founded in the 1950s by the Carpenter family, it quickly earned a worldwide reputation for its specialty coffee. All plants are Arabica, with Typica, Mundo Novo and Bourbon as the leading varieties, grown in volcanic soils at between 1,200 and 2,200 metres altitude.

Production is processed by the wet method, with strict quality control from manual harvesting through aerobic fermentation and drying. The result is what defines Sigri: perfect uniformity in the cup and a sensational balance between sweetness and acidity that few origins in the world can match.
